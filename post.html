Why I'm Not Making "Making of" Vlogs

Posted on Insert Date Zach, 2018

I've been thinking about this project for a few months at this point and I developed a real vision for a documentary style series that was going to entertainingly show my making of journey. After filming the first episode and a lot of reflection I've decided that it might not be this best way to go about it. There are a number of reasons for this the first comes from my last FMP. A lot of things went wrong in the run-up to my FMP last year but by the end of it all I was convinced that I had delivered a Distinction level project. I even won an award for my work and was told my numerous teachers that it was exceptional. I was certain that the only grade I deserved and would receive was a Distinction. However when I got my grade back I was disappointed I received a Merit with no explanation of why and no hope of finding one as my marker had left the school. I spent some time thinking about why I didn't get a Distinction and I came to the conclusion that my evaluation and research must of been the culprits.

The second part of this story comes when I came to watch back the footage of my first piece to camera in my vlog. Instead of seeing the well articulated and easy understanding evaluation I expected I saw a stuttering and convoluted mess. I managed to cut together a adequate evaluation but it was miles away from the slick video I'd been envisioning for months. It was then that I realised that maybe my presentation skills weren't up to the level I thought they where and that my vision may, for now, be out of reach, at least without investing far too much time.

Because of this I've decided that I'll save my time editing for my final product and I'll keep my evaluation for the most part written. I might make a behind the scenes video if I have the time but my vision is so far from my ability that I fear that my ambition was what hurt my grade the last time and could hurt my grade again.
